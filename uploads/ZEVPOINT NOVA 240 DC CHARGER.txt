DUAL-GUN DYNAMIC LOAD SHARING
------------------------------
The Nova 240 features advanced dual-gun architecture optimized for maximum throughput:

Dual CCS2 Ultra-High-Power Guns:
Two independent CCS2 charging guns with heavy-duty liquid-cooled cables rated for sustained high-current operation. Each gun capable of delivering up to 240kW when used individually. Both guns can operate simultaneously with intelligent dynamic power distribution.

Dynamic Load Sharing at Scale:
The Nova 240's sophisticated power management system dynamically allocates the 240kW total power based on real-time vehicle acceptance rates, battery temperatures, state of charge, and charging curve characteristics. The system optimizes for maximum total energy delivery and minimum total charging time across both guns simultaneously.

Mixed Vehicle Type Optimization:
When charging different vehicle types simultaneously (for example, a premium passenger car and an electric bus), the Nova 240 intelligently allocates power to maximize total throughput. A bus charging slowly at 80% state of charge may receive 80kW while a car charges at 160kW. As conditions change, power reallocation happens seamlessly in real-time.

Maximum Throughput Advantage:
Dual-gun capability with 240kW total power enables the Nova 240 to potentially serve twice as many vehicles per day compared to single-gun alternatives. Critical for high-traffic highway corridors and commercial operations where maximizing vehicles served per day directly determines revenue and business viability.

INSTALLATION REQUIREMENTS
--------------------------
The Nova 240 requires substantial industrial electrical infrastructure:

High-Capacity Electrical Power Supply:
Three-phase electrical supply mandatory. Input voltage typically 400V three-phase AC. Continuous 350-400 ampere capacity on input side required for full 240kW output plus system losses. Dedicated high-capacity circuit absolutely essential. Will require electrical service upgrade at most locations.

Industrial-Grade Electrical Infrastructure:
Very heavy-gauge cable from distribution panel - typically 95-150 square millimeter multi-core copper cable depending on distance. Four-pole MCB rated at 400A. Industrial-grade SPD rated for ultra-high-power installation. Heavy-duty RCD with DC fault detection rated for 240kW. Electrical panel upgrades likely required.

Maximum Earthing Requirements:
Ultra-robust earthing system with resistance below 1 ohm mandatory (0.5 ohms preferred). The 240kW power level requires exceptional earthing quality. Multiple chemical earthing electrodes typically required. Professional earth resistance testing and documentation essential for safety and insurance.

Substantial Foundation:
Heavily reinforced concrete foundation engineered for equipment weight plus environmental loads. Professional structural engineering may be required. Protective bollards rated for vehicle impact. Proper drainage and cable conduits. Foundation must comply with local building codes.

High-Speed Network:
High-speed reliable internet connection mandatory for OCPP network management. Dedicated fiber optic connection strongly preferred for commercial operations. Minimum 10-20 Mbps bandwidth with guaranteed uptime SLA. Redundant connectivity (fiber + 4G/LTE) recommended for critical revenue operations.

Substantial Physical Space:
Large clear space for vehicle queuing and maneuvering. Minimum 8-10 meters clear space in front. Wide lanes for buses and trucks. Separate queuing areas recommended for high-traffic locations. Adequate lighting for 24/7 operation. Signage and wayfinding.

Extensive Permitting and Approvals:
Electrical permit mandatory for 240kW installation. Professional electrical inspection required. Fire safety approvals especially for indoor or enclosed locations. Building permits for substantial foundation work. Utility company coordination - may require upgraded transformer or dedicated service. Environmental permits for some locations. Highway authority approvals for corridor locations.
